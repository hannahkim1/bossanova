The term "bossa nova" literally translates to “new trend” in Portuguese.

Originating in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil in the late 1950s, bossa nova has brought the unique cultures of Brazilian samba and American jazz together.

Bossa nova music relies on the singer’s nasally, yet sultry vocals and simple instruments, to create an iconic, gentle rhythm. A majority of bossa nova songs have minimalistic lyrics written in Portuguese, which portray stories of love and the slow paced daily life in Rio.

Bossa nova promotes an idea of paradise, a beautiful language with romantic imagery of vacationing on Copacabana beach. Bossa nova gives the listener a feeling of nostalgia- even if the listener has never experienced this unique lifestyle.

ORIGIN

Created in the 1950s, bossa nova became a Brazilian classic soon after. Bossa nova was loved throughout the 50s in Brazil, but it wasn’t until the early 60s when America joined the fun.

Bossa nova is not just a music genre, but is a representation of the Brazilian people’s general perspectives during a vital point in Brazilian history. The mid to late 1950s was a period of economic growth and democratic rule in between long periods of political instability. With the new leadership of President Juscelino Kubitschek de Oliveira, the people felt a sense of hope.

The middle class Brazilians in the Copacabana neighborhood were the backbone of bossa nova. The students and the struggling musicians who were regulars at the local jazz clubs had been influenced by American media, and were inspired to create a fresh sound.

Music clubs were exclusive, and live music was mostly only a luxury that was available to the upper class. Bossa nova created an opportunity for the lower-middle class to contribute to the music industry, immensely.

Later on, as bossa nova became more popular and mainstream, many were critical of the lack of class consciousness and alienation of actual Brazilian culture.

At this time, the Brazilian government began restricting musicians’ right to free speech. The government wanted the world to think of Brazil as the place to go if you want to experience this carefree life as presented in bossa nova.

Singing about beautiful women and bright, blue skies was not necessarily thought provoking. As this criticism for deeper-meaning music continued, bossa nova began to die down in Brazil; however, it continued gaining popularity in the US.

Tropicalia, beginning in the early 1960s, was heavily inspired by bossa nova and American rock and became the main form of protest from the restrictive government.

As Tropicalia was taking off, the US was absolutely loving bossa nova.

Frank Sinatra and Stan Getz were two of the biggest American artists to spread the bossa nova craze in America.

Sinatra recorded an album with legendary bossa nova artist, Antonio Carlos Jobim. Stan Getz recorded multiple bossa nova albums, including one with another legendary artist, Joao Gilberto.

Bossa nova’s success relied on the power of collaboration. In fact, bossa nova became so widely popular in the US, that the first ever bossa nova concert in the US took place at Carnegie Hall in 1962.

MUSIC

Bossa nova is a fusion of Brazilian dance samba rhythms and American smooth jazz harmonies.

It involves very few instruments, some of which include the guitar, berimbau (musical bow), surdo (bass drum), and piano, to emphasize that simplistic sound.

Bossa nova rhythm is a two bar pattern influenced by the syncopated claves in Afro-Cuban and traditional Brazilian samba music. This two bar pattern combined with a slower tempo creates a unique sound.Here is a short biography of some of the most important artists of the bossa nova movement.

ARTISTS

Here is a short biography of some of the most important artists of the bossa nova movement.

Tom Jobim

Born on January 25th, 1927, Antonio Carlos Jobim -born as Antonio Carlos Brasilerio de Almeida- was also famously known as Tom Jobim, was one of bossa nova's most influential artists.

Jobim was a composer, a singer, a songwriter, a player of many instruments. Jobim would play his music in jazz clubs in Rio before becoming the music director of one of the largest recording studios in Brazil, Odeon Records.

Jobim collaborated with many artists and produced wildly popular songs.

He is most known for creating one of the most popular songs, Garota de Ipanema (Girl from Ipanema) as well as the first ever bossa nova song, Chega de Saudade.

He has been incredibly influential to Brazilian music that the only international airport in Rio de Janeiro is named after him!

João Gilberto

Joao Gilberto, living from June 10th 1931 to July 6th of 2019, was often considered the father of bossa nova.

His album collaborations with Jobim solidified the building blocks of bossa nova style with his guitar rhythms and sultry songs.

He was married to bossa nova vocal powerhouse, Astrud Gilberto.

There are so many other notable and important artists that have influenced bossa nova, but Jobim, Joao and Astrud Gilberto are mostly considered the "golden trio" of bossa nova.

However, underneath the sunny and nostalgic image that is bossa nova, lies underlying racism and erasure of the African-Brazilian influence.

As I was researching and diving deeper into the true "influencers" of bossa nova, the “golden trio” were most credited.

But there was one name in particular that I had not recognized in all of my research until I encountered it whilst reading this article:

“Bossa Nova and white fear: Subtle mechanisms of racism erased Johnny Alf as the “Father” of one of the most important musical movements in Brazil”

Johnny Alf

Johnny Alf has been referred to as the real father of bossa nova.

Born as Alfredo Jose da Silva, he began by playing in small jazz clubs in the Copacabana neighborhood. He would play at Hotel Plaza, where Antonio Carlos Jobim frequently watched him perform.

In 1955, Alf’s song, Rapaz de Bem (Good Boy), was considered to be the first actual bossa nova song. In fact, Jobim told Alf that his hit song Desafinado was heavily inspired by Rapaz de Bem.

So why was it that Alf is not as well known, even though he was one of the first on the trend and one of the first to inspire some of the biggest names in bossa nova?

Simply put, it is because Johnny Alf was black, queer, and the son of a housemaid to a wealthy white family.

Alf did not fit the image that bossa nova wanted to promote.

Bossa nova is beautiful; however, it is crucial to discuss how it helps promote a euro-centric view of Brazilian culture, and how it silences the untold stories of those who had such a significant influence on Brazilian music culture.

NOW

New artists are embracing this unique sound.

TWICE

K-pop sensation girl-group, TWICE, released their title song “Alcohol Free” on their tenth mini album in 2021.

TWICE incorporating "latin inspirations" into their songs that catered to a Korean/international audience was absolutely incredible!

“Alcohol Free” includes elements of bossa nova- a refreshing and laidback take on their usual songs.

Billie Eilish

Grammy winner Billie Eilish released her song “Billie Bossa Nova'' in her Happier than Ever album in 2021.

It includes the classic lyrics of love and simple beats.

During an interview with Genius, Eilish talks about how her song was inspired by Antonio Carlos Jobim and his legacy that he left behind in the music world.

Coisa Mais Linda- TV Show

The Netflix show, Coisa Mais Linda, or Girls from Ipanema in the US, portrays a story of a young woman in the 50s from the big city of São Paulo as she breaks away from her kush, wealthy, yet strict family's rules and opens her own jazz club for her passion of bossa nova.

The show covers the lifestyles of different wealth classes as well as topics such as racism and sexism. Coisa Mais Linda has really beautiful shots of Rio with a great soundtrack and great cinematography.

Because the show is produced by Netflix, it introduced bossa nova to so many people, including myself.

It was a fun watch, and I recommend anyone who enjoys a casual tv show to give it a try.

PERSONAL

Bossa nova has changed my life. After watching Coisa Mais Linda in December of 2021, I immediately became enthralled by bossa nova.

I listen to bossa nova everyday. Whether I’m studying, getting ready for the day, or requesting a song request, it is all bossa nova.

I started to learn Portuguese and learn more about Brazilian culture. I use the Duolingo app everyday and have bought many books from my local Barnes and Noble.

Translating some of the songs' simple lyrics has helped me learn more Portuguese.

I understand that bossa nova does not represent the true culture of Brazil. Bossa nova does not even represent the entirety of Rio de Janeiro's culture either!

Brazil is an extremely diverse country that is more than just "beach, football, and beautiful women".

With that being said, I hope to one day travel to Brazil, and see live performances in music clubs with aspiring musicians.

I hope to experience the rich culture and legacy that bossa nova has left behind in Rio de Janeiro.

I have always had an admiration and respect for Brazil, and I hope to learn more and expand my knowledge of Brazilian culture in the future.
